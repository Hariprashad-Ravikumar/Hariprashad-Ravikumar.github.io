My PhD research focuses on extracting and analyzing the x (momentum fraction)-dependent Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distribution Functions (TMDs), such as Sivers observables, from lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) simulations using high-performance supercomputers. These TMD observables reveal the intrinsic motion of quarks and gluons inside the proton or neutron, specifically with respect to transverse momentum.

My other research involves interpolating the manifestly covariant conformal group SO(4,2) between the instant form and the light-front form of relativistic dynamics. One of the main goals of this work is to explore the advantages of using the light-front formulation over the instant form.
